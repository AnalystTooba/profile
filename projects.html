My expertise in Geographic Information (GI) systems has been developed through combination of GIS course lab reports, multiple design projects, and extensive hands-on experience across master planning, regional planning, and landscape planning projects. Additionally, my research work has further refined my analytical skills, enabling me to apply GI tools with precision and innovation.

Master Planning of Kasur

Master Planning is a year-long compulsory course focused on identifying urban challenges and devising effective solutions. I led the class and was responsible for GIS and cartographic tasks. For map preparation, I utilized advanced geospatial software, including Google Earth Engine, QGIS, ArcGIS Pro, and ArcMap. Once the data was collected, it was meticulously analyzed to uncover trends, patterns, and relationships among various land uses.

Vision

Kasur is envisioned to be a vibrant, inclusive, and sustainable and climate-resilient city that offers a high quality of life for all its residents.

Land Use and Land Cover Analysis (LULC)

With rapid urbanization LULC changes is considered as the most basic information in land management. Identifying changes in land use was crucial for addressing management and planning concerns within study area.

Land Use Change and Land Cover Trends Over Three Decades

LULC changes were analyzed focusing on five classes: agriculture, vegetation, urban/built-up, water, and bare land. The area covered by trees has demonstrated a declining trend, particularly evident from 2013 onwards.

Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) Assessment

Satellite-derived vegetation indices serve as valuable tools for tracking temporal changes in vegetation. Maps generated based on classification method was not defining the data accurately and stretched symbology is used instead for the classification purpose.

Conceptual Land Use Strategy and Future Development Zones

The Spatial Plan provides strategies to deliver environmental, social, and financial value to the city. The plan promotes economic growth and development while ensuring that natural assets like orchards and agricultural activities continue to provide resources and environmental services.

Distribution of Active and Passive Open Spaces within Kasur MC

The current open space in MC Kasur amounts to a total of 697.984 acres, with 432 acres designated as passive open space and 265.984 acres as active open space.

Core Area Density Map

A core density map helps identify areas with high population or activity concentrations, guiding efficient land use allocation, infrastructure development, and resource management in master planning.

Opportunity Map

Kasur offers facilities such as education, commerce, and jobs to its adjoining less urbanized cities and rural areas, maintaining efficient linkages with other cities through motorway and rail networks.

Night Light Map

Night light intensity can often correlate with economic activity. Brighter areas on the map might indicate higher concentrations of businesses, industries, or commercially active zones.

    Research Work

    This section showcases the Geographic Information (GI) analysis conducted as part of my extensive research work, encompassing my bachelor's thesis, International Fellowship, and German Spring School.

    Extracing Building Footprints from Google Earth Engine

    Extracting building footprints was crucial for carbon cooling models as it enables precise analysis of urban heat dynamics and green infrastructure planning for climate mitigation.

    Estimating Cooling Capacity (CC)

    I estimated the cooling capacity using the InVEST model, a tool developed by Stanford University students for ecosystem service analysis.

    Heat Mitigation Index (HMI)

    Calculating HMI was part of my thesis and it was essential for assessing the effectiveness of urban planning strategies in reducing heat stress.

    Proximity Analysis and Reclassification of Different Road Types

    Proximity analysis and reclassification of different road types using GI tools enable the assessment of infrastructure connectivity and guided in developing future scenarios.

    Developing Density (DENS) and Business-As-Usual (BAU) Scenario Development

    Developing DENS and BAU scenarios using Geographic Information (GI) tools is essential for spatially analyzing future urban growth and evaluating the effects of varying population densities and development strategies on land use and resources.

    Composite Image for Lahore District

    A composite image was formed by combining seven spectral bands, each representing different wavelengths of light, to create a multi-dimensional representation for detailed analysis.

    Land Surface Temperature (LST) Assessment for Lahore District

    LST was computed from Landsat imagery using thermal infrared bands, and temporal analysis was performed by comparing LST values across different time periods to assess temperature trends and changes.

    Land Use and Land Cover (LULC) Analysis for Lahore District

    LULC was computed by classifying satellite imagery using supervised classification techniques to categorize land into distinct cover types based on spectral characteristics.

    Normalized Difference Built-up Index (NDBI) for Lahore District

    NDBI was computed using the formula (SWIR - NIR) / (SWIR + NIR), where SWIR and NIR represent the short-wave infrared and near-infrared bands, respectively, to highlight built-up areas.
